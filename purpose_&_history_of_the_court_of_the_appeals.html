About the Court

PURPOSE

& HISTORY

The Court of Appeals of Indiana considers appeals from the state's trial courts
and selected administrative agencies. The Court of Appeals comprises of 15 judges
and senior judges, from the entire state. (Senior judges may also serve the court.)
Sitting in three-judge panels, they review trial court and state agency records
and usually answer a specific legal question or questions raised in the initial
trial or by the trial court's decision.

The 15 members of the court issued more than 1,900 written opinions (decisions)
in 2015. A Court of Appeals opinion is final unless granted further review
by the Indiana Supreme Court. The Court of Appeals does not preside over trials
and must accept all appeals as of right except those automatically sent to the
Indiana Supreme Court or the Indiana Tax Court.

Appeals from cases in which the sentence is the death penalty or life without
parole, appeals from trial court decisions finding a statute unconstitutional,
and attorney discipline cases are sent directly to the Indiana Supreme Court.

All opinions are available on the court's website.
