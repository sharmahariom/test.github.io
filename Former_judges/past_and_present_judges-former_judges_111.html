Past and Present Judges

FORMER JUDGES

Judge Ward Harrison Watson
Ward Watson was born on November 7, 1859 near Corydon in Harrison
County, Indiana. He attended Normal School in Danville, Indiana and
became a school teacher in Charlestown, Clark County. He studied law
in the office of James k Marsh, later his partner, and was admitted to the
bar in 1883. Judge Watson, known as one of the “Big Four” Republican
leaders in Clark County, practiced law there until his election as a
republicans to the Indiana Senate, representing Clark and Jefferson
counties. In 1906, he was elected to the Appellate Court and served one
term, from January 1, 1907 to January 1, 1911, From 1911 to 1931,
judge Watson practiced law in Indianapolis and remained active in
Republican party politics. He was a member of the Columbia Club in
Indianapolis and was trustee of Moores Hill College, serving a term as
President of its Board of Trustee. He married Edith Bamett in
Charlestown in 1890; she died in 1910. In 1931, he retired to his home
in Charlestown. In 1932, he was stricken with paralysis after watching
a horse show at the Kentucky State Fair in Louisville. He was driven to
his home in Charlestown and died shortly afterward, on September 19, 1932.
