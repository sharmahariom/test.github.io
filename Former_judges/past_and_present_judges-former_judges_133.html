Past and Present Judges

FORMER JUDGES

Judge Charles S. White
Charles S, White graduated from DePauw University and the former Voorhess Law School in Terre
Haute. He also received a law degree from the Indiana University School of Law in Indianapolis.
In 1938, he ran for Parke County Prosecutor. During World War II, he served in the Army Indianapolis
Corps.

Judge White was a public defender, an Assistant U.S. Attorney chief counsel for Indiana Attorney
General John J. Dillon, and as a Marion County criminal court judge. He was a partner at White
Maines & Mullen and at White & Saddler, both in Indianapolis.

Governor Roger Branigin appointed Judge White to the Indiana Appellate Court to Complete the term
of Judge Charles W. Cook, who died in office. Judge White took his seat on December17, 1968 and
served until his retirement from court on June 30, 1978.

Judge White was a member of the Indianapolis. Indiana State and American bar association the
Rosedale Masonic Lodge, the Indianapolis Jazz Club, the Indiana Historical Society, and All Souls
Unitarian Church.

He died on February 25, 1986 in Indianapolis at the age of 77. He was survived by his wife Elizabeth
Hurt White, and two daughter, Emile and Suzan.
