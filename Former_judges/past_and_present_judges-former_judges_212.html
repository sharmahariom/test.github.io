Past and Present Judges

FORMER JUDGES

Judge Milton Stapp Robinson
Milton Stapp Robinson was born in Versailles, Ripley County, Indiana on April 20,1832. He studied
law under his father Col. Joseph R. Robinson, a member of Indiana’s second constitutional
convention in 1851. Milton Robinson was admitted to the Bar in 1851 and opened a law practice in
Anderson, Indiana.
One of the first Republican in that party’s infancy, Judge Robinson was a president elector in 1856,
the first year the Republicans nominated a presidential candidate, John C. Fremont.
At the outbreak of the Civil War in 1861, Judge Robinson was a director of the Indiana State
Penitentiary at Michigan City. He resigned to enter the Union Army as a lieutenant colonel with the
47th Indiana, volunteer Infantry, receiving his Commission in September 1861. Later promoted to
colonel of the 75th Indiana, he commanded a regiment that fought in some of the Civil War’s most
pivotal battles, including the Battle of Chickamauga in Georgia in September 1863, Missionary Ridge
in Tennessee in November 1863, the siege of Atlanta during the summer of 1864, and General
Sherman’s march to the Sea. By the end of the war, Judge Robinson had risen to the rank of
brigadier–general.
Following the war, in 1866, Judge Robinson was elected to the state senate from grant and Madison
counties, where he served until 1870. In 1873, he founded the law firm of Robinson & Lovett in
Anderson. In 1874, he won election to Congress as a republican and served in the U.S. House of
Representatives from 1875 to 1879.
Robinson & Lovett became Robinson, Lovett & Kelner in 1888. In March 1891, the general Assembly
passed a statute creating a temporary Appellate Court, and Governor Alvin p. Hovey appointed judge
Robinson as one of the five original members.
Judge Robinson was a candidate for election to the Appellate Court at the time of his death. He died At home in Anderson on July 28, 1892.
