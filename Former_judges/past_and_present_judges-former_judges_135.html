Past and Present Judges

FORMER JUDGES

Judge Robert B. Lybrook
Robert B. Lybrook of Gary attended schools in Ohio and Indiana as a boy graduated
from Arsenal Technical High School in Indianapolis in 1932. He was journalism major at Indiana
University but graduated with a bachelor of laws degree in 1937. He was admitted to practice before
The Supreme Court of Indiana the same year.
He served in the United States Navy during World War II. Following the war, he returned to Indiana
and in 1945, began practicing in Franklin. He was Prosecuting Attorney for Johnson and Brown
counties from 1945 to 1950 and also served as county attorney for three years. From 1954 to 1967,
he was judge of the Circuit Court for Johnson and Brown Counties. He returned to his Law practice
and to his post as County attorney from 1967 until 1972, except for the three months he served
temporarily on the Appellate Court in 1970.
Governor Edger D. Whidcombe appointed Judge Lybrook to the Indiana Appellate Court in September
1970 to fill three months of the term of Judge James C. Cooper, who died in office. He returned to
private practice in 1971.
In 1971, under a new amendment to the Indiana Constitution, the Appellate Court became the Court of
Appeals of Indiana, and the Court expanded from eight to nine seats. Governor Whitecombe appointed
Judge Lybrook to the ninth seat on the court and he begin his work on January 1, 1972. In 1974, he
was retained on the court by election for a ten-year term. He retried 1979.
Judge Lybrook was a member of the Indiana Judges Association the American judicature Society, and
the Indiana State . Johnson County , Brown County and Morgan County Bar association. He was
Master of the Franklin Masonic Lodge, a member of the Masonic Lodge and Nashville, the Scottish Rite the Murat Shine, an Exalted Ruler
Of the Franklin Elks Club , and a member of the American Legion, Sigma Chi Fraternity Presbyterian Church. He was twice awarded a Sagamore of the Wabash.
He died on August 3, 1981 at the age of 67. He was survived by his wife Josephine E. Miller Lybrook and three children Robert, Jo Katherine and Mary Elizabeth.
