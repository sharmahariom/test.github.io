Judge Fred A. Wiecking
Frederick A. Wiecking was born in Bluffton, Indiana on August 15, 1892. During World War I
he served as a Lieutenant at Camp Hancock, Georgia. He received his law degree from
Indiana University in 1919 and did postgraduate work in Osnabruck, Germany. He was
admitted to the bar in Wells County in 1920, the same year married Marie White, and
practiced in the city of Bluffton for the next 12 years.
In 1932, Judge Wiecking moved to Hartford city to from the firm of Emshwiller and
Wiecking.
Governor Harry G. Leslie appointed Judge Wiecking, a Democrat, as Assistant Attorney
General of Indiana in 1933. In 1934, he graduated to the post of Public Counselor of

United States Senate.
Governor Paul V. McNutt appointed Judge Wiecking to the Indiana Appellate Court on
November 4, 1935 to fill the vacancy caused by the death of Judge Ralph Smith. He served
on the court until he died less than a year later.
At the time of his death Wiecking was an Ordinance Major on the staff of the
Indiana National Guard. Active in American Legion activities, he was the Indiana State
Commander in 1929. He was a member of the Elks, a former president of the Kiwanis Club,
a member of the Bluffton Chamber of Commerce, the Masons, and Sigma Alp[ha Epsilon ,
Fraternity.
He died suddenly at age 43, on July 28, 1936, while playing golf at the Highland Golf and
County Club in Indianapolis.
