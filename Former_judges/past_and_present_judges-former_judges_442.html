Past and Present Judges

FORMER JUDGES

Judge Robert H. Staton
Robert H. Staton was born in Indianapolis. He served for three years during World War II, from 1943 to
1946, with the 91st Infantry Division of the Fifth Army and was attached to a special contact force which
specialized in reconnaissance work behind the lines. He achieved the rank of major.
Following the war, he returned home and enrolled at Indiana University, where he received his
undergraduate degree in 1952. He received his law degree from Indiana University School of Law
Indianapolis in 1955. After graduation he spent three years as a Deputy Prosecuting Attorney,
Eventually becoming Chief Trial Deputy Prosecutor. He then entered private practice and founded
Staton & Ward.
Judge Staton was elected to the Indiana Appellate Court in November 1970, the last year election
Were held for his position. In 1971, under a new amendment to the Indiana Constitution, the Appellate
Court became the Court of Appeals of Indiana. Judge Staton was retained on the Court of Appeals by
election in three elections and served from January 1, 1971 until March 15, 2000. He authored more
than 3,000 majority opinions.
The first editor of Res Gestae, the law journal of the Indiana State Bar Association, Judge Staton
frequently publishes articles in legal journals. He has authored several state legal histories, including
“The History of the Court Appeals of Indiana” with Gina Hicklin in 1997, and ‘Lessons Learned: The
History of Continuing legal Education and Experience from Mandatory Continuing Legal Education
State’. He the Life Honorary Editor of the Indiana Law Review.
Judge Staton was President of the IU Alumni Association and served on the Executive Council for
Indiana university representation the law school, and on the school’s Alumni Association Board of
Directors from 2004 to 2007. The intramural Moot Court Competition at IU School of Law- Indianapolis
is named in his honor. He was chairman of the specialization committee and is a past president of the
Indiana State Bar Association and is a member of the American Bar Association.
He retired and lives in Indianapolis.
