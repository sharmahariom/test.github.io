FORMER JUDGES

Judge Paul H. Buchanan Jr.
Judge Name: Paul H. Buchanan, jr. was born in 1918 and attended the Park School in Indianapolis, now Park Tudor
School. He received his undergraduate degree from Swarthmore College in Pennsylvania in 1939. He
Entered graduate school in Cincinnati and then accepted a commission in the United States Navy.
During World War II he saw action in the South Pacific.
After the war he attended law school at the University of Arizona and the University of Denver. He
practiced in Indianapolis, becoming managing partner of Bose Buchanan McKinney & Evans.
Judge Buchanan was elected as Republican to the Appellate Court of Indiana in 1970 the last year
election were held for the position, in 1971 under a new amendment to the Indiana Constitution, the
Appellate Court became the Court of Appellate of Indiana. Judge Buchanan was retained on the Court
of Appeals by election in 1974 and 1984,in 1978, his colleagues selected him to the first of several
three year terms as Chief Judge.
For three decades Judge Buchanan was a contributing author to Res Gestae, the monthly publication
of the Indianapolis Bar Association. He was also a member of the House of Delegates for the American
Bar Association, the Indiana Supreme Court disciplinary commission, and the judicial nominating
Commission for the Marion County municipal courts. He served on the board of trustee of Methodist Hospital.
Judge Buchanan retired from the Court of Appellate in 1993 and lived in Indianapolis. With his sons, he
Owned Flanner & Buchanan funeral homes, one of the eldest family-owned businesses in Indiana,
Judge Buchanan died on November 6, 2008. He is survived by two sons, one daughter, and his
wife Ruth Buchanan.
