Born in Canton, Georgia, Justice Rocker grew up in Gary, Indiana and is a veteran of the Vietnam War.
He is a graduate in Indiana University (B.A. 1974) and Valparaiso University School of Law (J.D. 1976).
In 1998, he earned a Master of Laws degree in the judicial process from the University of Virginia Law
School.

Prior to his appointment to the Indiana Supreme Court, Justice Rucker served as a judge on the
Indiana Court of Appeals, having been appointed to that position in 1991 by Governor Evan Bayh.
While on the Court of Appeals, Justice Rucker served as vice-chair of the Indiana Commission for
Continuing Legal Education. As a lawyer, Justice Rucker served on the board of directors of the Indiana
Trial Lawyers Association and on the board of directors of the Northwest Indiana legal Services
Organization. He also served as a deputy prosecuting attorney for Lake County, City Attorney for the
City of Gray, and engaged in the general practice of law in East Chicago.

Justice Rocker is a member of the American Bar Association, the Indiana Judges Association, the
Indiana State Bar Association, the Marion County Bar Association, and is a Fellow of the Indianapolis
Bar Foundation. Justice Rucker also serves as Chair-elect of the Judicial Council of the National Bar
Association.

Justice Rocker is married and has two sons and a daughter.
