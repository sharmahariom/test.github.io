FORMER JUDGES

Judge Moses B. Lairy
Moses B. Lairy served the Indiana judiciary at the circuit, appellate and Supreme
Court level. He was born in Cass County, Indiana on August 13, 1859 and attended
Valparaiso University. He became a school teacher, teaching from 1878 to 1888.
During summer breaks, he studied law in the offices of McConnell in
Logansport. He enrolled in the law Department of the University of Michigan, and
graduated with an L.L.B in 1889. He returned to Class County, was admitted to the
bar, and later married local resident Nina Justice.
Governor Claude Matthews appointed Judge lairy to a vacancy on the cass circuit
Court in 1895 and he served for one year. He then practiced law in Logansport and
served a learn as Cass County Attorney.
In 1910, Judge Lairy was elected as a Democrat to the Appellate Court and served
one term from January 1, 1911 to January 1, 1915. He was elected as a Judge of the
Indiana Supreme Court in 1914 and served on the Supreme Court from January 4,
1915 to January 3, 1921.
Following his retirement from the bench, he practiced law in Lafayette for a year with
Clyde H. Jones, then joined with former governor Samuel Ralston to from the
Indianapolis firm of Ralston, Gates, Lairy, Van Nuys and Barnard. He defended Judge
Clarence W. Dearth of Mason and an Elk, and a member of the Indiana and American
Bar Association the Indianapolis Athletic Club. The Highland Golf and Country Club.
and the Indianapolis Chamber of Commerce. He died suddenly while visiting relatives
near Logansport on April 9, 1927.
