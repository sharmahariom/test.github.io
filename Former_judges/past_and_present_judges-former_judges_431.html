Past and Present Judges

FORMER JUDGES

Judge John M. Ryan
John M. Ryan was born in 1920 in Indianapolis. He served in the United States Navy during
World War II as a flight Instructor. He graduated from the University of Notre Dame before
Receiving his law degree from Indiana University School Law.

Judge Ryan served as the City Prosecutor for the city of Indianapolis from 1951 to 1955. He
Was appointed to the Superior Court Judgeship in 1956 by Governor George N. Craig. He went
in to the private practice of law in 1958.
Judge Ryan was appointed to the Indiana appellate Court on March, 11 1959, beginning his
Service on the court five days later. He was appointed with Judge Russell Smith to one of two
Newly created judgeships on the Appellate Court by Governor Harold W. Handley. In 1961,
Judge Ryan was elected to a four-year term on the court ending on December 31, 1961. He
served as Chief Judge during the November 1961 term. In 1968, Judge Ryan was elected to
a four-year term on the court, and he served until December 31, 1973.

Judge Ryan also served as a State Senator and Parliamentarian of the Indiana Senate from
1974 to 1980.From 1980 to 1984 he served as special counsel for the Honorable Robert D. Orr
Governor of Indiana, Judge Ryan became the Marion County Circuit Court judge in 1984 and
Remained in that position until 1992.

Judge Ryan died on July 7, 2006. He was survived by his wife and two children.
