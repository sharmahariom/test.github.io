FORMER JUDGES

Judge Betty Barteau
Betty Barteau was born Boonville, Warrick County, Indiana. She Attended Indiana University law School
at Indianapolis, graduating with an L.L.B. in 1965. She was admitted to the Indiana Bar that
same year. She served as Boonville City Judge, Deputy Prosecutor in Spencer and Warrick Counties,
and Warrick County Attorney. She had a general law practice in Marion County from 1969 to 1974,
before becoming a Marion County Superior Court Judge in 1975.

Judge Barteau served as a judge on the Court of Appeals of Indiana from January 1991 to April 1998.
She left the Court to assume the director position for the Russian American judicial partnership, and
USAID program based in Mascow. She served in that position until 2003.

Judge Barteau has held memberships in various professional and community organization and has received
numerous awards and recognition for her work in both areas.
