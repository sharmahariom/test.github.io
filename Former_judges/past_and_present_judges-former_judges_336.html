Past and Present Judges

FORMER JUDGES

Judge Dewey E. Kelley
Dewey E. Kelley was born June 10, 1898 in Memphis, near Jeffersonville in Clark County. The youngest of
Three boys, his father died when he was two, and his mother and brothers moved first to new Albany, then
Scottsburg then Cambridge City. He graduated from high school in Cambridge City in 1917.
A few month following his high school graduation, he enlisted in the U.S. Army, one
Survive gassing in the trenches and the other was killed in battle. Following his honorable discharge, judge
Kelley worked on construction of a concrete road from Richmond to Indianapolis and in the yards of the
Pennsylvania Railroad at Bradford, Ohio.
Judge Kelley graduated from the Valparaiso University Law School in 1922, though he was admitted to the
Wayne County Bar in 1921. He first practiced law in Frankfort, unsuccessfully by his own account, but in
Frankfort met and married Roxie Yount on August 9, 1924. Together they moved to Kokomo, then to Whiting.
While he and Roxie raised two daughters, Barbara and Betsy, Judge Kelley served as the attorney for the
Whiting School Board and as president of the Whiting Bar Association. Beginning in 1940, he was a member
Of the Advisory Board for Registrants in the U.S. Selective Service System, and during World War II, he was
appointed to the War price Control and Rationing Program and as chairman of the Writing office of price
Administration. In addition, he was elected judge of the Whiting city Court in 1942, a post he had until
January 1, 1948.
In 1952, Judge Kelley was elected to the Indiana Appellate Court. He won reelection in 1956 and 1960 and
Served on the court until December 31, a1964. He was Chief Judge in the November 1954, November 1957,
May 1958, and May 1962 terms. He ran unsuccessfully for re-election in 1964.
Judge Kelley was a member of the American, Indiana State, Whiting and Hammond bar associations the Veterans of foreign Wars the American
Legion. Veterans of World War I Tau Kappa Epsilon fraternity, Whiting Lodge of free and Accepted Masons the Ancient Accepted lodge of
Perfection of the Scottish Rite the Ancient Arabic Order of Nobles of the Mystic, Shine, The Loyal Order of Moose and the Indiana Recording
Club.He was both a Sagamore of the Wabash and Kentucky Colonel.
