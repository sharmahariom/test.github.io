Past and Present Judges

FORMER JUDGES

Judge John C. McNutt
John Chrittenden McNutt was born in Johnson County, Indiana on May 25, 1863.
At 17, he became a schoolteacher and studied law under his uncle, Judge Cyrus
McNutt in Terre Haute. He was admitted to the bar in Johnson County in his early
20s and in 1886 he began practicing law with William C. Thompson in Franklin. He
was elected to a two- year term as prosecuting attorney for Johnson and Shelby
counties in 1888.
In 1893, he left Franklin to serve as State Law Librarian, an office he held for five
Years. Afterward, he practiced law in Martinsville.
Judge McNutt was named to the Indiana Appellate Court by Governor Samuel M.
Ralston replace Judge Joseph h. Shea, who resigned to become U.S. Ambassador
To Chile. Judge McNutt took his seat on May1, 1916 and served until January 1, 1917.
He ran as a Democrat for election to the court in 1916 but was unsuccessful.
Following his tenure on the court, he returned to Martinsville to practice law. He helped
organize the First National Bank of Martinsville, serving on its, board of directors, and
was a Charter member of the Indiana State Bar Association.
His only child, Paul V. McNutt, practices law with his father for a time in Martinsville.
Paul McNutt served as Governor of Indiana from 1933 to 1937.
Following the death of his Ruth in 1947. Judge McNutt moved to New York City to
live with his son. He died there in December 1949.
