Past and Present Judges

FORMER JUDGES

Judge Wesley W. Ratliff, Jr
Wesley W. Ratliff, Jr. was born in September 1925 in Knightstown. He attended Ball State University,
completing two years before joining the united States Navy in December 1943. During World War II
he served in the practice, assisting in training on Midway.

Following his military service, he returned to Ball State to finish his degree. He received his law degree
From Valparaiso University School of Law in January 1950 and was admitted to practice in May of that
year. He returned to Knightstown to practice with his parents, both lawyers.

He served as deputy prosecutor for Henry County in 1953. He was elected as the Circuit Judge of
Henry County as a Republican in 1952, succeeding his further who had died in 1961. Judge Ratliff was
re-elected to Circuit Court, running unopposed in 1959 and 1974.

Judge Ratliff was appointed to the Court of Appeals of Indiana by Governor Otis Bowen and took his
Seat on January 1, 1980. He was retained on the Court by election in 1982. He served as Chief judge
of the Court from 1987 to 1992. He retired from the court in November 1992. He taught at Valparaiso
University School of Law in the fall of 1984 and at Indiana University School of Law- Indianapolis in
1987.

Judge Ratliff continued to live in Knightstown until 1987 when he and his wife Mildred moved to
Bloomington. He was active in many legal and civic organizations in both Knightstown and
Bloomington.

Judge Ratliff served as a senior Judge on the court from his retirement until his death on August 30,
2006. He was survived by his wife and three children.
