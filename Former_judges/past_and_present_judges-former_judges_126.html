FORMER JUDGES

Judge Frank Hamilton
Frank Hamilton was born near Clarksburg in Decatur County on April 2, 1883, He Graduate from Clarksburg
High School in 1900 and attended Butler University for one year before completing his degree at Indiana
University. He received his law degree from the Indiana Law School of Indianapolis in 1905.
On December 14, 1907. He married Mary Frances lsgrigg with whom he had two sons William and Frank
He was admitted to the Bars of Decatur County and the Indiana Supreme Court in June 1905. He practiced
law in Greensburg from 1905 to 1929, beginning with a year in the office of Tackett & Wilson, then from 1907
to 1912 as the junior member of Ewing & Hamilton. In 1912 he formed his own firm with two partners, Osborn
Hamilton and Harding later Osborn Hamilton. He and John Osborn were then joined by Judge Hugh
Wickens to from Wicknes, Osbom & Hamilton which later became Wickens & Hamilton.
Judge Hamilton served as Decatur County Deputy Prosecuting Attorney from 1907 to 1909 and Greensburgâ€™s
City Attorney from 1919 to 1929. He was Secretary of the Decatur County Republican Central Committee from 1910 to 1912.
In 1930, he moved to Terre Haute to Practice law, first with Hamill, Hickey & Hamilton then as a solo
Practitioner and with his son William, While residing in Terre Haute, Judge Hamilton served on the
Republican Board of Election Commission of Virgo County. He was Counsel for the School Board of
Terre, Haute for a year in 1944.In 1943 he was appointed first Deputy Attorney General under
Attorney General James a Emmert, Who later became a member of the Indiana Super Court.Judge Hamilton
was elected as a Republican to the Indiana Appellate Court and took his seal on January 1, 1945. He served for one four year term
including as Chief Judge for the May 1946 session. He was nominated by the Republican Party of Reelection but was defeated in 1948
general Election. After learning the Court Judge Hamilton returned to Greensburg to Practice law specializing in Appellate Procedure.
His son frank joined the firm in 1951. Judge Hamilton was appointed to the Indiana Supreme Court Disciplinary Commission, where he
served until his death. Judge Hamilton a member of the Clarksburg Masonic Lodge and the Greensburg Presbyterian, Church. He was
past Exalted Ruler of the ELKS After a brief illness he died in Indianapolis on February 10, 1952 at the age of 68.
