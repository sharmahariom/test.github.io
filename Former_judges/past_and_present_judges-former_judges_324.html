FORMER JUDGES

Judge Alonzo Lee Nichols
Alonzo Lee Nichols was born in Lynn, Indiana on August 3, 1856. He attended the
State normal School at Terre Haute for one year, and While still a teenager became
A schoolteacher in his hometown.
In 1880 he married kittie Wiseman and moved the short distance to Winchester. He
Taught school there for six years, and in 1885 served a term as deputy Randolph
County clerk. During this experience, he took up the study of law in the offices of
Thompson, Marsh, and Thompson and was admitted to the bar in 1889. He practiced
For nearly 20 years in Winchester. In 1892, he was elected as Randolph County clerk.
Judge Nichols was elected as a Republican of three terms on the Indiana Appellate
Court, beginning in 1918, and served from January 1, 1919 to January 1, 1931. He
was Chief Judge Five times-for the May 1919, May 1922, November 1922, November
1925 and May 1928 terms. After his years on the court, Judge Nichols returned to
Private practice, in Winchester, with his son Merrill.
For more than 30 years, Judge Nichols taught Sunday School at the Main street
Church of Christ in Winchester, where he served on the Board of Deacons. He also

him in death. He lost his wife in 1935 and remained a year and a half later, to olive
Van Nuys. He died at home on June 9, 1937.
