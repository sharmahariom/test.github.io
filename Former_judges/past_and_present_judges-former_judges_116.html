Past and Present Judges

FORMER JUDGES

Judge Joseph Hooker Shea
Joseph H. Shea was born in Lexington, Scott County, Indiana on July 24, 1863. He
studied law in Scottsburg under Charles Jewett and was admitted to the bar in 1885.
He then entered Indiana University and graduated in 1889 with a Bachelor of Arts.
He returned to Scottsburg and practiced law for nearly 10 years with Col. Jewett and
Mark Storen, in 1882 he became a member of the Board of trustees of Indiana
University.

Very active in Democratic Party politics, Judge Shea was elected as a Democrat to
the Indiana Senate in 1896, serving Scott, Clark and Jennings counties. In 1898, he
moved to Seymour and formed a partnership with Cart E. Wood, From 1900 to 1904,
he was the prosecutor for Scott, Jennings, and Ripley counties. He was elected Judge
of the 40th judicial circuit in 1906.

In 1912, Judge Shea was elected to the Indiana Appellate Court. He served on the
court from January 1, 1913 until the spring of 1916, on May 1, 1916, he was
appointed by president Woodrow Wilson as U.S. Ambassador to Chile, a post he held
from May 30, 1916 to May 5 1921.
