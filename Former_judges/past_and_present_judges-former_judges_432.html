FORMER JUDGES

Judge Warren W. Martin
Warren W. Martin was born in1892. A World War I veteran Judge Martin received his undergraduate
Degree from the University of Louisville and his law degree from the Jefferson School of Law, an
Independent right school that several years later was absorbed into the University Of Louisville School
Of Law. He was admitted to practice before the Indiana Supreme Court, the U.S. District Court for the
Southern District of Indiana and the U.S. Supreme Court.
In 1924, he began his public service career after his election as Warrick County Prosecutor. Serving for
Two term until 1928 from 1929 until 1934, he was Boonville City Attorney.
For six years, Judge Marlin served as Deputy Attorney General. Governor Henry F. Schricker
Appointed him Chairman of the Indiana industrial Boards in 1941, a post he held for the duration of
World War II.
He ran for election to the Indiana Appellate Court and served from January 1, 1949 to December 31,
1952. He was Chief Judge during the May 1952 term, he was unsuccessful in the bid for re-election in 1952.
Judge Martin returned to the State Industrial Board as a minority member from 1957 to 1961. From
1961 until his second election to the Appellate Court, he served as public Counselor for the state public
service Commission.
He was member of the Indiana State bar Association the Masonic Lodge of Kentucky the Indiana
Democratic Club and the main street Methodist Church of Boonville Governor Mathew Welsh named
him a Sagamore of the Wabash in 1961.
Judge Martin was elected again to the Appellate Court in 1964. He died after a heart attack on his home
In Beech grove on June 25, 1965 after serving just six months of his new term on the court.
Judge’s Martin first wife Hester Landwehr Martin died in 1936. He later married Elizabeth Gregory,
who survived her husband as did Judge Martin’s six sons and seven daughter.
