FORMER JUDGES

Judge John W. Pfaff
John W. Pfaff was born in Marietta, Ohio in 1900 and moved with his family to Indiana in his
youth. He graduated from Hanover College in 1926 and from the Indianapolis law School
in 1929. He began a general practice of law in South Bend and served on the staff of the
U.S. District Attorney.

Judge Pfaff was first elected to the Indiana Appellate Court in 1954 and served three
nonconsecutive terms; from January 1, 1955 to December 31, 1970. He served as
Chief Judge of the Appellate court during the May 1955, May 1961,and May 1967 terms
and during the year 1969.

Judge Pfaff was a member of the Indiana State and St. Joseph County bar associations, a
past member of the Free and Accepted Masons in portage, a 32nd degree Mason, and a
member of Beta Theta Pi fraternity, the Scottish Rite, Murat Temple Shrine the Kiwanis
Club, Ridge date Presbyterian Church, and was active in the Community Chest, the Red
Cross, and the Boy Scouts.

He died on February 27, 1977 at the age of 76. He was survived by his widow Odessa and
their two sons, John And Robert.
