About the Court

HISTORY

The concept of a second appellate-level court to serve the entire State of Indiana
originated with an enactment of the Indiana General Assembly in 1891. The
Appellate Court of Indiana was created to relieve the growing caseload of the
Indiana Supreme Court; it was intended to exist as a judicial panel for six years to
expedite an overflow of appellate cases. In 1897, the court was retained for an
additional four years. In 1901, the court was made the permanent Appellate Court,
which evolved into an eight-member court that sat in panels comprised of four
judges. Eventually, the eight-member Appellate Court was abolished and today's
Court of Appeals of Indiana was created as a constitutional court by an
amendment to the Indiana Constitution ratified on November 3, 1970. The court
came into existence on January 1, 1972.
