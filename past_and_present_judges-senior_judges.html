SENIOR JUDGES

THE ROLE OF SENIOR JUDGES

Senior judges are experienced members of the Court of Appeals, and with their
accumulated insight and wisdom, they are a valuable resource. The court uses
their expertise to help reduce caseload burdens.
Court of Appeals judges must retire at age 75. Retired judges may seek senior judge
status and are required to work at least 30 days per year.
Senior judges are assigned every 16th case from the civil, criminal, and expedited
case rotations. They serve on three-judge panels and on the court's motions panel.
